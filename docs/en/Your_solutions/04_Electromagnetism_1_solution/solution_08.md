# Task 08 – Lorentz Force

## Problem Statement

A charged particle has

$$
q = 2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}
$$

$$
m = 4 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}
$$

It enters a magnetic field

$$
B = 0.5 \text{ T}
$$

with speed

$$
v = 10^6 \text{ m/s}
$$

perpendicular to the field.

Determine the magnitude of the Lorentz force.

## Theory

For motion perpendicular to a magnetic field, the magnetic force magnitude is

$$
F = qvB
$$

since

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the given values:

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)
$$

First combine the powers of ten:

$$
2 \times 10^{-19} \cdot 10^6 = 2 \times 10^{-13}
$$

Now multiply by $0.5$:

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

The Lorentz force magnitude is

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, the magnetic force reaches its maximum possible value for this speed and field strength.
