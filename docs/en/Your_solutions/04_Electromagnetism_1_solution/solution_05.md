# Task 05 – Field Levitation

## Problem Statement

Determine the electric field required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity.

Use:

$$
m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}
$$

$$
g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

## Theory

For levitation, the upward electric force must balance the downward gravitational force.

The electric force is

$$
F_e = qE
$$

The gravitational force is

$$
F_g = mg
$$

At equilibrium:

$$
qE = mg
$$

Thus,

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For a proton:

$$
m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
q = e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}
$$

So,

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

First compute the numerator:

$$
mg = 1.6366 \times 10^{-26}
$$

Then divide:

$$
E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}
$$

## Final Result

The required electric field magnitude is

$$
E \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}
$$

The field must point upward, so that the proton experiences an upward electric force.

## Interpretation

Because the proton is extremely light, only a very small electric field is required to balance its weight. This illustrates how weak gravity is compared with electromagnetic effects on elementary charged particles.
