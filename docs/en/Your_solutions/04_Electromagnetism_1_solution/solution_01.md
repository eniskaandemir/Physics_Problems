# Task 01 – Coulomb's Law

## Problem Statement

Four point charges of

$$
+1.0 \text{ C}
$$

are placed at the corners of a square of side length

$$
a = 1.0 \text{ m}
$$

A charge

$$
q_0 = -2.0 \text{ C}
$$

is placed at the center of the square.

Determine the magnitude and direction of the total electric force acting on the central charge.

## Theory

The electric force between two point charges is given by Coulomb's law:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

where

$$
k = 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N m}^2/\text{C}^2
$$

and $r$ is the distance between the charges.

The force is attractive if the charges have opposite signs and repulsive if they have the same sign.

When several charges act on a particle, the net force is the vector sum of all individual forces.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Geometry of the Square

The central charge is at the center of the square.

The distance from the center to any corner is half of the diagonal:

$$
r = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

With $a = 1.0$ m,

$$
r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \text{ m}
$$

Thus,

$$
r^2 = \frac{1}{2}
$$

### 2. Force from One Corner Charge

Each corner charge is

$$
q = +1.0 \text{ C}
$$

The center charge is

$$
q_0 = -2.0 \text{ C}
$$

The magnitude of the force from one corner is

$$
F_1 = k \frac{|q q_0|}{r^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F_1 = 8.99 \times 10^9 \frac{(1.0)(2.0)}{1/2}
$$

$$
F_1 = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot 4
$$

$$
F_1 = 3.596 \times 10^{10} \text{ N}
$$

Each force points from the center toward a corner, because the interaction is attractive.

### 3. Vector Symmetry

There are four identical positive charges placed symmetrically around the center.

Therefore:

- the force from the upper-right corner is canceled by the force from the lower-left corner
- the force from the upper-left corner is canceled by the force from the lower-right corner

Thus the vector sum of all four forces is

$$
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \vec{0}
$$

## Final Result

The total force on the central charge is

$$
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \vec{0}
$$

Its magnitude is

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 0
$$

## Interpretation

Although each corner charge exerts a large attractive force on the central negative charge, the arrangement is perfectly symmetric. As a result, all horizontal and vertical components cancel, so the net electric force at the center is zero.
