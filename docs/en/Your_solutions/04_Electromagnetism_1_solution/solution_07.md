# Task 07 – Cyclotron Motion

## Problem Statement

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of

$$
V = 5000 \text{ V}
$$

It then enters a uniform magnetic field

$$
B = 0.1 \text{ T}
$$

perpendicular to its velocity.

Determine the radius of the circular path.

## Theory

When a charge is accelerated through a potential difference $V$, the gained kinetic energy is

$$
eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

In a uniform magnetic field perpendicular to the velocity, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:

$$
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
$$

Thus,

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Find the Speed of the Electron

Use

$$
eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}
$$

Substitute

$$
e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}
$$

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
V = 5000 \text{ V}
$$

Then

$$
v = \sqrt{
\frac{2(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(5000)}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}
}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Find the Radius

Use

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The radius of the circular path is

$$
r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

The magnetic field bends the electron into circular motion without changing its speed. Because the electron has very small mass, the radius is also very small.
