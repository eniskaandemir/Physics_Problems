# Task 08 – Circular Motion

## Problem Statement

Find the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

Earth radius:

$R = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

---

## Theory

Centripetal acceleration:

$$
a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}
$$

Velocity from rotation:

$$
v = \frac{2\pi R}{T}
$$

where $T = 24 \text{ h} = 86400 \text{ s}$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Linear Speed

$$
v = \frac{2\pi R}{T}
$$

$$
v = \frac{2\pi \cdot 6.378\times 10^6}{86400}
$$

$$
v \approx 465 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### 2. Centripetal Acceleration

$$
a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}
$$

$$
a_c = \frac{465^2}{6.378\times 10^6}
$$

$$
a_c \approx 0.034 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
a_c \approx 0.034 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

## Interpretation

This acceleration is very small compared to gravitational acceleration ($9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$), which is why Earth's rotation is not directly felt.
