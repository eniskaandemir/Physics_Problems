# Task 05 – Relative Velocity

## Problem Statement

A river flows east at

$$
2 \text{ m/s}
$$

A boat has speed

$$
5 \text{ m/s}
$$

relative to the water.

The river is $200$ m wide.

Determine:

- the direction the boat must head to move directly north
- the time required to cross

---

## Theory

Relative velocity:

$$
\vec{v}_{\text{ground}} = \vec{v}_{\text{boat}} + \vec{v}_{\text{river}}
$$

To move straight north, the horizontal components must cancel.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Horizontal Cancellation

River velocity:

$$
v_r = 2
$$

Boat must provide opposite horizontal component:

$$
v_{bx} = -2
$$

---

### 2. Use Velocity Magnitude

Boat speed:

$$
v_b = 5
$$

Thus:

$$
v_b^2 = v_{bx}^2 + v_{by}^2
$$

$$
25 = 4 + v_{by}^2
$$

$$
v_{by}^2 = 21
$$

$$
v_{by} = \sqrt{21}
$$

---

### 3. Angle of Motion

$$
\sin\theta = \frac{v_{by}}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{21}}{2}\right)
$$

---

### 4. Crossing Time

Only vertical motion matters:

$$
t = \frac{200}{v_{by}}
$$

$$
t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.6 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Final Result

Angle:

$$
\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{21}}{2}\right)
$$

Time:

$$
t \approx 43.6 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The boat must aim upstream to cancel the river's flow.  
This ensures the resulting motion is purely northward relative to the ground.
