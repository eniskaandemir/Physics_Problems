# Task 11 – Young's Two-Slit Interference

## Problem Statement

Simulate Young’s double-slit experiment using two coherent wave sources:

$$
u(\vec{r},t) = \frac{A}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r_1}|} \sin(k |\vec{r} - \vec{r_1}| - \omega t) + \frac{A}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r_2}|} \sin(k |\vec{r} - \vec{r_2}| - \omega t)
$$

Determine how slit distance and wavelength affect the interference pattern.

## Theory

The total wave is the sum of two coherent sources.

Constructive interference occurs when:

$$
\Delta r = n\lambda
$$

Destructive interference occurs when:

$$
\Delta r = \left(n + \frac{1}{2}\right)\lambda
$$

where $\Delta r$ is the path difference.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Superposition

The resulting wave field is

$$
u_{\text{total}} = u_1 + u_2
$$

### 2. Interference Pattern

- bright fringes → constructive interference
- dark fringes → destructive interference

### 3. Effect of Parameters

#### Slit distance $d$

- larger $d$ → fringes closer together
- smaller $d$ → fringes spread out

#### Wavelength $\lambda$

- larger $\lambda$ → wider spacing
- smaller $\lambda$ → tighter pattern

### 4. Visualization

The intensity pattern emerges from summing wave contributions at each point.

## Final Result

The interference pattern depends on:

- wavelength
- slit separation
- distance from slits

## Interpretation

This experiment demonstrates the wave nature of light and sound. The interference pattern arises purely from superposition.
