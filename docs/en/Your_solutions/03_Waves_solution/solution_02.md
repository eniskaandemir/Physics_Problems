# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string has length

$$
L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}
$$

Its fundamental frequency is

$$
f_1 = 330 \text{ Hz}
$$

Determine the speed of the wave on the string.

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental mode has one antinode and satisfies

$$
L = \frac{\lambda_1}{2}
$$

Thus,

$$
\lambda_1 = 2L
$$

Wave speed is then

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For the fundamental mode:

$$
\lambda_1 = 2L
$$

Substitute $L = 0.64$ m:

$$
\lambda_1 = 2(0.64)
$$

$$
\lambda_1 = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

Now use the wave relation:

$$
v = f_1 \lambda_1
$$

$$
v = 330 \cdot 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The wave speed on the string is

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

In the fundamental mode, the string length corresponds to half a wavelength. Once this wavelength is known, the wave speed follows directly from the frequency.
