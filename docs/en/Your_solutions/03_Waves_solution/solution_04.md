# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

Two points on a wave are separated by a distance

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Determine the phase difference in radians.

## Theory

The phase difference between two points separated by distance $\Delta x$ on a sinusoidal wave is

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

into the phase formula:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\cdot\frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Cancel $\lambda$:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference is

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad}
$$

## Interpretation

A separation of one-third of a wavelength corresponds to one-third of a full cycle. Since one full cycle is $2\pi$ radians, the phase difference is $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians.
