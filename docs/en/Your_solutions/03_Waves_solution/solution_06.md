# Task 06 – Wave Equation

## Problem Statement

A wave is described by

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05\sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

Determine:

1. amplitude $A$
2. wavelength $\lambda$
3. frequency $f$
4. wave speed $v$

## Theory

The standard form of a traveling wave is

$$
y(x,t) = A\sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

where

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

and

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

The wave speed is

$$
v = \frac{\omega}{k} = f\lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Compare

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05\sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

with

$$
y(x,t) = A\sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

Thus:

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

$$
k = 2\pi
$$

$$
\omega = 50\pi
$$

### Wavelength

Use

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

So

$$
2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

### Frequency

Use

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

Thus,

$$
50\pi = 2\pi f
$$

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

### Wave Speed

Use either formula:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

$$
v = 25 \cdot 1 = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

Amplitude:

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

Wavelength:

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

Frequency:

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

Wave speed:

$$
v = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The equation directly reveals the wave parameters by comparison with the standard sinusoidal wave form. The coefficient in front of the sine gives the amplitude, while the coefficients of $x$ and $t$ determine wavelength and frequency.
