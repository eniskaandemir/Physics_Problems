# Task 07 – Standing Wave Modes

## Problem Statement

A standing wave with four antinodes is produced on a string of length

$$
L = 80 \text{ cm} = 0.80 \text{ m}
$$

Determine the wavelength of the wave.

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the allowed standing-wave modes satisfy

$$
L = n\frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

where $n$ is the number of antinodes.

Here,

$$
n = 4
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Use

$$
L = n\frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Substitute $L = 0.80$ m and $n = 4$:

$$
0.80 = 4\frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

$$
0.80 = 2\lambda
$$

$$
\lambda = \frac{0.80}{2}
$$

$$
\lambda = 0.40 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda = 0.40 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Four antinodes correspond to the fourth harmonic. In this mode, the string length contains four half-wavelength segments.
