# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has frequency

$$
f = 440 \text{ Hz}
$$

The speed of sound is:

- in air: $v_{\text{air}} = 343 \text{ m/s}$
- in water: $v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \text{ m/s}$

Determine the wavelength in air and in water.

## Theory

For any wave, the relation between speed, frequency, and wavelength is

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

Solving for wavelength gives

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

When a wave enters a different medium, its frequency remains the same, but its speed and wavelength change.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Wavelength in Air

Use

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{v_{\text{air}}}{f}
$$

Substitute the given values:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

### Wavelength in Water

Use

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

Wavelength in air:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \text{ m}
$$

Wavelength in water:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Because sound travels much faster in water than in air, the wavelength is much larger in water. The frequency stays fixed at $440$ Hz because it is determined by the source.
