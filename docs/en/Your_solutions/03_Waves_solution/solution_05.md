# Task 05 – Echo Ranging

## Problem Statement

A person shouts toward a cliff and hears the echo

$$
1 \text{ s}
$$

later.

The speed of sound in air is

$$
v = 343 \text{ m/s}
$$

Determine the distance to the cliff.

## Theory

The measured time includes:

- the trip from the person to the cliff
- the return trip from the cliff back to the person

Thus, the total sound path is twice the one-way distance.

Use

$$
d_{\text{total}} = vt
$$

and then divide by $2$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The total distance traveled by the sound is

$$
d_{\text{total}} = vt
$$

$$
d_{\text{total}} = 343 \cdot 1
$$

$$
d_{\text{total}} = 343 \text{ m}
$$

Since this is the round-trip distance, the one-way distance to the cliff is

$$
d = \frac{343}{2}
$$

$$
d = 171.5 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The cliff is

$$
171.5 \text{ m}
$$

away.

## Interpretation

The sound travels to the cliff and back, so the measured time must be halved when determining the actual distance to the reflecting surface.
