# Task 06 – Energy Dissipation

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0$ m. After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy.

Determine the height reached after the second bounce.

## Theory

The mechanical energy at maximum height is purely gravitational:

$$
E = mgh
$$

If the ball loses $30\%$ of its energy after each bounce, it retains $70\%$ of its energy. Therefore, after each bounce,

$$
E_{\text{new}} = 0.70 E_{\text{old}}
$$

Since $E = mgh$ and both $m$ and $g$ remain constant, height is directly proportional to mechanical energy:

$$
h_{\text{new}} = 0.70 h_{\text{old}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Initial Height

The initial height is

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Height After the First Bounce

The ball keeps $70\%$ of its energy, so the first rebound height is

$$
h_1 = 0.70 h_0
$$

$$
h_1 = 0.70 \cdot 2.0
$$

$$
h_1 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

### 3. Height After the Second Bounce

Again the ball keeps $70\%$ of the energy from the previous bounce:

$$
h_2 = 0.70 h_1
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.70 \cdot 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

Equivalently,

$$
h_2 = (0.70)^2 \cdot 2.0 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The height after the second bounce is

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the ball loses the same fraction of mechanical energy after each bounce, the rebound heights form a geometric sequence. Each new height is $70\%$ of the previous one.
