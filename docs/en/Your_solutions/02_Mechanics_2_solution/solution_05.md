# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision

## Problem Statement

A $70$ kg runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140$ kg stationary cart.

Determine:

1. the final speed of the cart and runner
2. whether kinetic energy is conserved

## Theory

In a perfectly inelastic collision, the objects stick together after impact. Linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Kinetic energy is not generally conserved in inelastic collisions. It must be checked by calculating the initial and final values.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Apply Momentum Conservation

Runner:

$$
m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}, \qquad v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}
$$

Cart:

$$
m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}, \qquad v_2 = 0
$$

After the runner lands on the cart, the total mass is

$$
m_1 + m_2 = 210 \text{ kg}
$$

Use momentum conservation:

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Check Kinetic Energy

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2 v_2^2
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$,

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2)
$$

$$
K_i = 35 \cdot 9
$$

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}
$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2
$$

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2)
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Since

$$
K_f \neq K_i
$$

kinetic energy is not conserved.

## Final Result

Final speed:

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

Kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}, \qquad K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Therefore, kinetic energy is not conserved.

## Interpretation

Momentum is conserved because external horizontal forces are neglected during the collision. Kinetic energy decreases because some of it is converted into heat, sound, and internal deformation when the runner lands on the cart.
