# Task 12 – Transformer Currents

## Problem Statement

A transformer has:

$$
N_p = 1000
$$

primary turns and

$$
N_s = 200
$$

secondary turns.

The primary voltage is

$$
V_p = 120\,\text{V}
$$

The secondary current is

$$
I_s = 3\,\text{A}
$$

Determine:

1. the secondary voltage
2. the primary current

## Theory

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p}
=
\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

Current relation:

$$
\frac{I_s}{I_p}
=
\frac{N_p}{N_s}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Secondary Voltage

Use:

$$
V_s
=
V_p
\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

Substitute:

$$
V_s
=
120
\cdot
\frac{200}{1000}
$$

$$
V_s
=
24\,\text{V}
$$

### 2. Primary Current

Use:

$$
I_p
=
I_s
\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

Substitute:

$$
I_p
=
3
\cdot
\frac{200}{1000}
$$

$$
I_p
=
0.6\,\text{A}
$$

## Final Result

Secondary voltage:

$$
V_s = 24\,\text{V}
$$

Primary current:

$$
I_p = 0.6\,\text{A}
$$

## Interpretation

The transformer reduces the voltage because the secondary coil has fewer turns. To conserve power approximately, the current changes inversely with voltage.
