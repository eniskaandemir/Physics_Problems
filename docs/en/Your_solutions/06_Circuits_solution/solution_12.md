# Task 12 – Transformer Currents

## Problem Statement

A transformer has primary turns:

$$ N_p = 1000 $$

and secondary turns:

$$ N_s = 200 $$

The primary voltage is:

$$ V_p = 120\,\text{V} $$

The secondary current is:

$$ I_s = 3\,\text{A} $$

Determine:

1. the secondary voltage
2. the primary current

## Theory

For an ideal transformer:

$$ \frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p} $$

For currents:

$$ \frac{I_s}{I_p} = \frac{N_p}{N_s} $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Secondary Voltage

$$ V_s = V_p \frac{N_s}{N_p} $$

$$ V_s = 120 \cdot \frac{200}{1000} $$

$$ V_s = 24\,\text{V} $$

### 2. Primary Current

$$ I_p = I_s \frac{N_s}{N_p} $$

$$ I_p = 3 \cdot \frac{200}{1000} $$

$$ I_p = 0.6\,\text{A} $$

## Final Result

$$ V_s = 24\,\text{V} $$

$$ I_p = 0.6\,\text{A} $$

## Interpretation

The transformer steps down voltage because the secondary coil has fewer turns. In an ideal transformer, lower voltage corresponds to higher current on the secondary side.
