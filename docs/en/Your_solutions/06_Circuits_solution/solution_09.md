# Task 09 – Current

## Problem Statement

Charge flowing through a wire is:

$$ Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5 $$

Determine the current at:

$$ t = 3\,\text{s} $$

## Theory

Current is the rate of change of charge:

$$ I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt} $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Differentiate the charge function:

$$ Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5 $$

$$ I(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5) $$

$$ I(t) = 10t $$

Evaluate at $t=3$:

$$ I(3) = 10(3) $$

$$ I(3) = 30\,\text{A} $$

## Final Result

$$ I(3) = 30\,\text{A} $$

## Interpretation

The current increases linearly because the charge function is quadratic in time.
