# Task 09 – Current

## Problem Statement

The electric charge flowing through a wire is described by

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Determine the current at time

$$
t = 3\,\text{s}
$$

## Theory

Electric current is the rate of change of charge:

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Differentiate the charge function:

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Therefore,

$$
I(t)
=
\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5)
$$

$$
I(t)
=
10t
$$

Now evaluate at

$$
t = 3\,\text{s}
$$

$$
I(3)
=
10(3)
$$

$$
I(3)
=
30\,\text{A}
$$

## Final Result

The current at

$$
t = 3\,\text{s}
$$

is

$$
I = 30\,\text{A}
$$

## Interpretation

The current increases linearly with time because the charge function grows quadratically. A faster increase in charge corresponds to a larger electric current.
