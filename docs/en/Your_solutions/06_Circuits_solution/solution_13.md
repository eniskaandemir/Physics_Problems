# Task 13 – Transformer Ratio

## Problem Statement

A transformer steps down voltage from

$$
120\,\text{V}
$$

to

$$
9.0\,\text{V}
$$

The primary coil has

$$
N_p = 400
$$

turns.

Determine the number of turns in the secondary coil.

## Theory

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p}
=
\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

Solve for the secondary turns:

$$
N_s
=
N_p
\frac{V_s}{V_p}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute values:

$$
N_s
=
400
\cdot
\frac{9.0}{120}
$$

$$
N_s
=
400
\cdot
0.075
$$

$$
N_s
=
30
$$

## Final Result

The secondary coil must have

$$
N_s = 30
$$

turns.

## Interpretation

A smaller number of secondary turns produces a lower secondary voltage. The transformer ratio directly determines how voltage changes between the coils.
