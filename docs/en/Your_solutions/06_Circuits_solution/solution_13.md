# Task 13 – Transformer Ratio

## Problem Statement

A transformer steps down voltage from:

$$ V_p = 120\,\text{V} $$

to:

$$ V_s = 9.0\,\text{V} $$

The primary coil has:

$$ N_p = 400 $$

Determine the number of turns in the secondary coil.

## Theory

For an ideal transformer:

$$ \frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p} $$

Solve for $N_s$:

$$ N_s = N_p \frac{V_s}{V_p} $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute values:

$$ N_s = 400 \cdot \frac{9.0}{120} $$

$$ N_s = 400 \cdot 0.075 $$

$$ N_s = 30 $$

## Final Result

$$ N_s = 30 $$

## Interpretation

The secondary coil needs fewer turns because the transformer is stepping the voltage down.
