# Task 11 – Power and Energy

## Problem Statement

A resistor has resistance

$$
R = 100\,\Omega
$$

A voltage of

$$
V = 50\,\text{V}
$$

is applied across it.

Determine:

1. the power dissipated
2. the energy consumed in 5 minutes

## Theory

### Electrical Power

For a resistor:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

### Electrical Energy

Energy is related to power by

$$
E = Pt
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Power

Use:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
P
=
\frac{50^2}{100}
$$

$$
P
=
\frac{2500}{100}
$$

$$
P
=
25\,\text{W}
$$

### 2. Energy in 5 Minutes

Convert time to seconds:

$$
5\,\text{min}
=
300\,\text{s}
$$

Now calculate energy:

$$
E = Pt
$$

$$
E
=
25 \times 300
$$

$$
E
=
7500\,\text{J}
$$

## Final Result

Power dissipated:

$$
P = 25\,\text{W}
$$

Energy consumed in 5 minutes:

$$
E = 7500\,\text{J}
$$

## Interpretation

The resistor converts electrical energy into heat. The longer the resistor operates, the more energy is dissipated.
