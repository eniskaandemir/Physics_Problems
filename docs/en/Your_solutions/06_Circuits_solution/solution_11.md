# Task 11 – Power and Energy

## Problem Statement

A resistor has resistance:

$$ R = 100\,\Omega $$

A voltage is applied:

$$ V = 50\,\text{V} $$

Determine:

1. the power dissipated
2. the energy consumed in $5$ minutes

## Theory

Power dissipated in a resistor:

$$ P = \frac{V^2}{R} $$

Energy consumed:

$$ E = Pt $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Power

$$ P = \frac{V^2}{R} $$

$$ P = \frac{50^2}{100} $$

$$ P = \frac{2500}{100} $$

$$ P = 25\,\text{W} $$

### 2. Energy

Convert time:

$$ 5\,\text{min} = 300\,\text{s} $$

Calculate energy:

$$ E = Pt $$

$$ E = 25 \cdot 300 $$

$$ E = 7500\,\text{J} $$

## Final Result

$$ P = 25\,\text{W} $$

$$ E = 7500\,\text{J} $$

## Interpretation

The resistor converts electrical energy into heat. The energy consumed increases linearly with time.
