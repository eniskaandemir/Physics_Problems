# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors are connected in parallel to a battery of voltage

$$
V = 10\,\text{V}
$$

The capacitances are

$$
C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}
$$

$$
C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}
$$

Determine:

1. the total charge stored
2. the total energy stored

## Theory

### Parallel Capacitors

For capacitors in parallel:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2
$$

### Charge Stored

The total charge is

$$
Q = CV
$$

### Energy Stored

The energy stored in a capacitor is

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Equivalent Capacitance

Add the capacitances:

$$
C_{\text{eq}}
=
4\,\mu\text{F}
+
6\,\mu\text{F}
$$

$$
C_{\text{eq}}
=
10\,\mu\text{F}
$$

Convert to SI units:

$$
C_{\text{eq}}
=
10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}
$$

### 2. Total Charge

Use:

$$
Q = CV
$$

Substitute values:

$$
Q
=
(10 \times 10^{-6})(10)
$$

$$
Q
=
1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C}
$$

### 3. Total Energy

Use:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

Substitute values:

$$
U
=
\frac{1}{2}
(10 \times 10^{-6})
(10)^2
$$

$$
U
=
\frac{1}{2}
(10 \times 10^{-6})
(100)
$$

$$
U
=
5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}
$$

## Final Result

Equivalent capacitance:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\text{F}
$$

Total charge stored:

$$
Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C}
$$

Total energy stored:

$$
U = 5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}
$$

## Interpretation

In a parallel capacitor arrangement, the capacitances add directly because each capacitor experiences the same voltage. Increasing total capacitance increases both the stored charge and the stored energy.
