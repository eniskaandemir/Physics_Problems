# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors are connected in parallel to a $10$ V battery.

$$ C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F} $$

$$ C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F} $$

$$ V = 10\,\text{V} $$

Determine:

1. the total charge stored
2. the total energy stored

## Theory

For capacitors in parallel:

$$ C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2 $$

Charge stored:

$$ Q = CV $$

Energy stored:

$$ U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Equivalent Capacitance

$$ C_{\text{eq}} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} $$

$$ C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\text{F} $$

Convert to farads:

$$ C_{\text{eq}} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F} $$

### 2. Total Charge

$$ Q = CV $$

$$ Q = (10 \times 10^{-6})(10) $$

$$ Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C} $$

### 3. Total Energy

$$ U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 $$

$$ U = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(10)^2 $$

$$ U = 5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J} $$

## Final Result

$$ C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\text{F} $$

$$ Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C} $$

$$ U = 5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J} $$

## Interpretation

In parallel, capacitances add directly because each capacitor experiences the same voltage.
