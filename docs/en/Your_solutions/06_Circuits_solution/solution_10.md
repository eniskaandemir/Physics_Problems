# Task 10 – Average Current

## Problem Statement

A lightning bolt transfers a charge of

$$
Q = 30\,\text{C}
$$

to the ground in a time interval of

$$
\Delta t = 2\,\text{ms}
$$

Determine the average current.

## Theory

Average current is defined as

$$
I_{\text{avg}}
=
\frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert milliseconds to seconds:

$$
2\,\text{ms}
=
2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{s}
$$

Now use the average current formula:

$$
I_{\text{avg}}
=
\frac{30}{2 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
I_{\text{avg}}
=
1.5 \times 10^4\,\text{A}
$$

## Final Result

The average current of the lightning bolt is

$$
I_{\text{avg}}
=
1.5 \times 10^4\,\text{A}
$$

## Interpretation

Lightning transfers an enormous amount of charge in a very short time interval, producing extremely large electric currents.
