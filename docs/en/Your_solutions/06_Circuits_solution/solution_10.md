# Task 10 – Average Current

## Problem Statement

A lightning bolt transfers charge:

$$ Q = 30\,\text{C} $$

The time interval is:

$$ \Delta t = 2\,\text{ms} $$

Determine the average current.

## Theory

Average current is:

$$ I_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t} $$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert milliseconds to seconds:

$$ 2\,\text{ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{s} $$

Apply the formula:

$$ I_{\text{avg}} = \frac{30}{2 \times 10^{-3}} $$

$$ I_{\text{avg}} = 1.5 \times 10^4\,\text{A} $$

## Final Result

$$ I_{\text{avg}} = 1.5 \times 10^4\,\text{A} $$

## Interpretation

Lightning produces a very large current because a large amount of charge is transferred in a very short time.
