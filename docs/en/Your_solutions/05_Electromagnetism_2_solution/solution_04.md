# Task 04 – Magnetic Torque

## Problem Statement

A rectangular current loop has dimensions

$$
10 \text{ cm} \times 5 \text{ cm}
$$

and carries current

$$
I = 2 \text{ A}
$$

A uniform magnetic field of magnitude

$$
B = 0.3 \text{ T}
$$

is applied parallel to the plane of the loop.

Determine the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop.

## Theory

The magnetic torque on a current loop is

$$
\tau = \mu B \sin\theta
$$

where $\mu$ is the magnetic dipole moment

$$
\mu = IA
$$

and $\theta$ is the angle between the area vector of the loop and the magnetic field.

If the magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop, then it is perpendicular to the area vector. Therefore,

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

and

$$
\sin\theta = 1
$$

So the torque becomes

$$
\tau = IAB
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert Dimensions to Meters

$$
10 \text{ cm} = 0.10 \text{ m}
$$

$$
5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Calculate the Area

$$
A = (0.10)(0.05)
$$

$$
A = 0.005 \text{ m}^2
$$

### 3. Compute the Torque

Use

$$
\tau = IAB
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\tau = (2)(0.005)(0.3)
$$

$$
\tau = 0.003 \text{ N m}
$$

## Final Result

The magnetic torque on the loop is

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N m}
$$

## Interpretation

The torque is maximum because the magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop, which means it is perpendicular to the loop's area vector. The field tends to rotate the loop so that its magnetic moment aligns with the field.
