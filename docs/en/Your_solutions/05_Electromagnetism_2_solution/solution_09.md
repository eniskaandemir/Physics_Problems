# Task 09 – Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Problem Statement

A ray of light travels from air into glass.

Refractive indices:

$$
n_1 = 1.00
$$

$$
n_2 = 1.50
$$

Angle of incidence:

$$
\theta_1 = 30^\circ
$$

Determine the angle of refraction.

## Theory

Snell's law relates the angles and refractive indices:

$$
n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2
$$

Solve for $\theta_2$:

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{n_1}{n_2}\sin\theta_1
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values:

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{1.00}{1.50}\sin 30^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\sin 30^\circ = 0.5
$$

we get

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{1.00}{1.50} \cdot 0.5
$$

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{1}{3}
$$

Now take the inverse sine:

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)
$$

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

## Final Result

The angle of refraction is

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

## Interpretation

Because light enters a medium with higher refractive index, it slows down and bends toward the normal. Therefore, the refracted angle is smaller than the incident angle.
