# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement

Determine the speed of light in diamond, whose refractive index is

$$
n = 2.42
$$

## Theory

The speed of light in a medium is related to its refractive index by

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

where

$$
c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

is the speed of light in vacuum.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Use

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of light in diamond is

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Diamond has a large refractive index, so light travels through it much more slowly than in vacuum. This large slowing also contributes to the strong bending and optical brilliance associated with diamonds.
