# Task 07 – Wavelength and Frequency

## Problem Statement

The human eye is most sensitive to light of wavelength

$$
\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}
$$

Determine:

1. the corresponding color in the visible spectrum
2. the frequency of this light

## Theory

Visible light around

$$
550 \text{ nm}
$$

corresponds to green light.

The frequency is related to wavelength by

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

where

$$
c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert Wavelength to Meters

$$
550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda = 5.50 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Compute Frequency

Use

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{5.50 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Final Result

The color is

$$
\text{green}
$$

The frequency is

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Interpretation

A wavelength near $550$ nm lies near the middle of the visible spectrum, which is why it corresponds to green light. This region is also close to the peak sensitivity of the human eye.
