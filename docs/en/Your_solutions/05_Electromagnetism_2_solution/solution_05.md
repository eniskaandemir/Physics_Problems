# Task 05 – Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Problem Statement

A parallel-plate capacitor has:

$$
S = 0.02 \text{ m}^2
$$

$$
d = 5 \text{ mm} = 5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

$$
V = 500 \text{ V}
$$

Determine:

1. the capacitance $C$
2. the stored energy
3. the electric field intensity $E$
4. the force of attraction between the plates

## Theory

For a parallel-plate capacitor in vacuum or air,

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

The stored energy is

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

The electric field between the plates is

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

The attractive force between the plates can be found from the electric pressure relation

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Capacitance

Use

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

with

$$
\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}
$$

Substitute:

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \frac{0.02}{5 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

First simplify the fraction:

$$
\frac{0.02}{5 \times 10^{-3}} = 4
$$

Thus,

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 4
$$

$$
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}
$$

### 2. Stored Energy

Use

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(3.54 \times 10^{-11})(500)^2
$$

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(3.54 \times 10^{-11})(2.5 \times 10^5)
$$

$$
U = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}
$$

### 3. Electric Field

Use

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{5 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ V/m}
$$

### 4. Force Between the Plates

Use

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

Substitute:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(1.0 \times 10^5)^2(0.02)
$$

Since

$$
(1.0 \times 10^5)^2 = 1.0 \times 10^{10}
$$

we obtain

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(1.0 \times 10^{10})(0.02)
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85 \times 10^{-2})(0.02)
$$

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

Capacitance:

$$
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}
$$

Stored energy:

$$
U = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}
$$

Electric field:

$$
E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ V/m}
$$

Attractive force:

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$

## Interpretation

A parallel-plate capacitor stores energy in the electric field between its plates. The smaller the separation, the stronger the electric field and the greater the attractive force between the plates for a fixed voltage.
