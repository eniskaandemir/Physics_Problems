# Task 02 – Ampere's Law

## Problem Statement

Two long parallel wires are separated by

$$
d = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.10 \text{ m}
$$

Each wire carries a current of

$$
I = 5 \text{ A}
$$

in opposite directions.

Determine the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at the point midway between the wires.

## Theory

The magnetic field produced by a long straight wire at distance $r$ is

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

where

$$
\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T m/A}
$$

The direction is determined by the right-hand rule.

Because the midpoint is equidistant from both wires, the fields due to the two wires have equal magnitudes. Since the currents are opposite, the magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the same direction and therefore add.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Distance from Each Wire to the Midpoint

The midpoint lies halfway between the wires, so

$$
r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{0.10}{2} = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Field from One Wire

Use

$$
B_1 = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5)}{2\pi(0.05)}
$$

Simplify:

$$
B_1 = \frac{20\pi \times 10^{-7}}{0.1\pi}
$$

$$
B_1 = 2.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

### 3. Total Field

The two contributions are equal and point in the same direction, so

$$
B_{\text{tot}} = 2B_1
$$

$$
B_{\text{tot}} = 2(2.0 \times 10^{-5})
$$

$$
B_{\text{tot}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

### 4. Direction

Using the right-hand rule for opposite currents, the magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the same direction.

If one wire carries current upward and the other downward, then at the midpoint both fields point either into the page or out of the page, depending on which wire is on which side.

For the standard arrangement with the left wire upward and the right wire downward, the field at the midpoint points

$$
\text{into the page}
$$

## Final Result

The magnetic field magnitude at the midpoint is

$$
B = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

Its direction is perpendicular to the page, and for the standard left-up, right-down arrangement it points

$$
\text{into the page}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the currents are opposite, the magnetic fields between the wires reinforce each other instead of canceling. This produces a stronger field at the midpoint than from one wire alone.
