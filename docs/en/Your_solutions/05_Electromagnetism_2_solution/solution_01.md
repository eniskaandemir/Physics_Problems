# Task 01 – Gauss's Law

## Problem Statement

A point charge

$$
q = +2 \text{ C}
$$

is located at the origin.

Determine the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius

$$
R = 1 \text{ m}
$$

centered at the origin.

## Theory

Gauss's law states that the total electric flux through any closed surface is

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

where:

- $\Phi_E$ is the electric flux
- $q_{\text{enc}}$ is the enclosed charge
- $\varepsilon_0$ is the permittivity of free space

$$
\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2/(\text{N m}^2)
$$

For a point charge at the center of a spherical Gaussian surface, the flux depends only on the enclosed charge, not on the radius.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The spherical surface encloses the full charge

$$
q_{\text{enc}} = +2 \text{ C}
$$

Apply Gauss's law:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.85 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{C}
$$

## Final Result

The electric flux through the spherical surface is

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{C}
$$

## Interpretation

The flux is positive because the enclosed charge is positive, so electric field lines leave the surface outward. The value does not depend on the radius of the sphere, only on the total enclosed charge.
